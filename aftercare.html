Eyebrow aftercare

After a brow design, tint or lamination, your skin and hairs need a little time to settle. As a general guide:

  • Keep the area dry and avoid touching it for the first 24 hours.
  • Avoid makeup, sunbathing and swimming pools for 24–48 hours.
  • Brush lamination brows into shape with a clean spoolie each morning.
  • Avoid picking or over-plucking between appointments; let your artist tidy stray hairs instead.

Lash aftercare

Lash lifts, tints and extensions all benefit from a gentle first 24–48 hours:

  • Keep lashes dry and avoid steam, saunas or hot showers for the first day.
  • Avoid oil-based cleansers and makeup removers, which can loosen the treatment early.
  • Sleep on your back where possible for the first night or two.
  • Brush gently with a clean spoolie if lashes look ruffled.

Micropigmentation healing

Semi-permanent brows go through a few visible stages while they heal. This is completely normal, and every skin type heals a little differently:

  • Brows often look bolder and darker for the first few days, softening as healing progresses.
  • Light flaking or peeling is a normal part of healing. Let it happen naturally; do not pick.
  • Keep the area clean, dry and free from makeup, sun and sweat while it heals.
  • Colour continues to soften and settle over the following weeks, with a top-up appointment recommended to perfect the result.

You will be given specific, written aftercare instructions for your skin and treatment at your appointment: always follow those over any general guidance.

Makeup advice

A few general tips to help any makeup application go the distance, event or everyday:

  • Arrive with clean, moisturised skin, since makeup always sits better on well-prepped skin.
  • Avoid sun exposure the day before an event to keep your base even.
  • Blot rather than wipe during the day to keep makeup intact.
  • For long days, ask about long-wear or setting products at your consultation.

Treatment preparation

A little preparation makes a real difference to your results:

  • Arrive with clean, makeup-free brows and lashes where possible.
  • Let your artist know about any sunbathing, sunbeds or sunburn beforehand.
  • A patch test is required at least 48 hours before your first tint, lamination or micropigmentation appointment.
  • Avoid caffeine before lash treatments if you know it makes you fidgety, since a still face gets the best result.

Healing timelines

As a general guide only (your own timeline may vary), and you will get specifics for your treatment on the day:

  • Tints & waxing: settled within 24 hours, lasting 2–4 weeks.
  • Lamination & lash lifts: fully set after 24 hours, lasting 6–8 weeks.
  • Micropigmentation: initial healing over 7–10 days, full colour settle over 4–6 weeks, with a top-up typically recommended after.

Do’s and don’ts

Do

  • Follow the specific aftercare card given at your appointment.
  • Keep the treated area clean and dry for the first 24 hours.
  • Reach out if anything feels wrong: a quick message is always welcome.

Don’t

  • Pick, scratch or over-cleanse the treated area.
  • Book a spray tan, sunbed or swim within 24–48 hours.
  • Apply makeup, oils or retinol-based products until fully healed.

FAQs

Is redness or sensitivity normal?

A little redness straight after waxing, tinting or micropigmentation is normal and usually settles within a few hours to a day. If anything feels more than mild, get in touch.

Can I wear makeup straight after my appointment?

It is best to avoid makeup on the treated area for at least 24 hours, longer for micropigmentation. Your artist will confirm timings for your specific treatment.

What if I am not happy with my results?

Get in touch as soon as possible. Many concerns are a normal part of the healing process and settle with time, but it is always better to check in than to wait and worry.
